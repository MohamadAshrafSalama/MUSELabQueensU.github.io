MUSE Lab at Queen's University

Human-centred software engineering research.

The Moving Intelligent User-Centric Software Engineering (MUSE) Lab studies how people, tools, and communities shape software systems. The lab combines empirical software engineering, human-computer interaction, and machine learning to improve the sustainability, inclusivity, and productivity of complex socio-technical ecosystems.

Research Directions

Questions at the intersection of software, people, and communities.

Each theme reflects a core strand of the lab's research program and anchors future projects, publications, and student work.

Empirical Software Engineering

Evidence-driven studies of how software systems are built, maintained, and evolved.

Open-Source Software

Understanding sustainability, participation, governance, and tooling in OSS communities.

Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion

Designing more inclusive pathways for participation in software engineering.

Machine Learning for SE

Studying how data-driven systems can support developers while preserving human judgment.

Developer Productivity

Investigating how developers define, experience, and improve productive work.
